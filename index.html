Cryptocurrency mixers deliberately obfuscate the origin of USDT TRC20 funds by pooling and redistributing tokens to break the on-chain trail. OFAC and FinCEN treat mixer interaction as a significant AML red flag. What is a peeling chain and can TRXRiskScan detect it on TRON?+
A peeling chain is a money laundering technique where USDT TRC20 is moved through a long series of TRON wallet addresses in rapid succession — sending a small amount to a "peel" address each time to obfuscate the trail. TRXRiskScan's graph analysis engine detects peeling chain patterns on TRON by identifying linear transaction sequences with characteristic output splitting ratios, flagging them as structural money laundering indicators in the TRX risk report.

What is KYT and how does it relate to TRX risk scanning?+
KYT (Know Your Transaction) is the blockchain compliance practice of monitoring and analyzing individual cryptocurrency transactions rather than just end-user identities. For TRX and USDT TRC20, KYT involves real-time screening of every deposit or withdrawal to identify risk flags, sanctions exposure and unusual fund flow patterns. TRXRiskScan provides KYT functionality for TRON by scoring both wallet addresses and transaction hashes — enabling exchanges to apply KYT across their entire USDT TRC20 flow in real time.

What is a VASP and what TRX risk obligations does it have?+
A Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) is any business that exchanges, transfers or custodies cryptocurrencies including USDT TRC20 or TRX. Under FATF Recommendation 15, VASPs must implement AML programs including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring and sanctions screening. For TRON-based operations, this means running TRX risk checks on every deposit address, applying the Travel Rule to USDT TRC20 transfers above threshold amounts and maintaining audit-ready risk records — all of which TRXRiskScan facilitates.
What is the TRON Travel Rule and how does TRXRiskScan support it?+
The FATF Travel Rule requires VASPs to collect and transmit originator and beneficiary information for cryptocurrency transfers above $1,000 — including USDT TRC20 transactions on TRON. TRXRiskScan supports Travel Rule compliance by enabling pre-transaction screening of TRON counterparty wallets, identifying whether a receiving TRX address belongs to a regulated VASP or unhosted wallet and generating the documentation needed for Travel Rule matching systems used by TRON ecosystem participants.
Can TRXRiskScan detect structuring (smurfing) in USDT TRC20 transactions?+
Yes. Structuring involves breaking large USDT TRC20 transactions into many smaller amounts to avoid detection thresholds. TRXRiskScan's behavioral analysis engine detects structuring patterns on TRON by identifying clusters of same-day, similarly-sized transactions from coordinated TRX wallet networks. These patterns are flagged as high-risk indicators and contribute to the overall risk score for the analyzed address, making TRXRiskScan an effective tool for smurfing detection on TRON.

What is round-tripping in TRON and how is it detected by TRXRiskScan?+
Round-tripping is a TRON money laundering technique where USDT TRC20 funds are sent through a chain of wallet addresses and eventually return to a wallet controlled by the same actor, creating the appearance of legitimate trading activity. TRXRiskScan's graph analysis engine identifies round-trip patterns by mapping closed-loop fund flows in the TRON transaction graph where output paths converge back to known input clusters — a strong structural TRX risk red flag.

What is TRX address clustering and why does it matter for risk analysis?+
TRX address clustering is the process of grouping multiple TRON wallet addresses that are likely controlled by the same entity based on shared spending patterns, common inputs and behavioral signatures. TRXRiskScan uses advanced clustering algorithms to identify address clusters on TRON — meaning that even if funds are split across many TRX wallets, the risk score reflects the aggregate behavior of all wallets in the cluster. This prevents risk evasion through address rotation and provides a far more accurate USDT TRC20 compliance picture.
