What is TRX Risk Scan (TRXRiskScan)?+
TRX Risk Scan (TRXRiskScan) is a professional blockchain risk intelligence and AML compliance platform built specifically for the TRON network. It allows anyone — from individual users to enterprise exchanges — to instantly scan any TRX wallet address, TRON transaction, or TRC20 address against global sanctions lists, illicit fund databases, mixer exposure reports, and behavioral risk models to receive a comprehensive TRX risk score and detailed compliance report in under 1 second.

Paste any TRX wallet address (beginning with "T") into the Risk Scan widget at the top of this page and click Scan. What is a TRX risk score and how is it calculated?+
The TRXRiskScan risk score is a number from 0 to 100. Zero means fully clean with no illicit exposure; 100 means a direct sanctions match or confirmed illicit TRX fund source. The score is calculated using a multi-factor algorithm weighing direct exposure to sanctioned TRON entities, indirect counterparty exposure across up to 10 transaction hops, interaction with known TRON mixers or tumblers, presence of darknet market TRX transactions, and behavioral clustering patterns associated with money laundering on TRON.
Does TRXRiskScan check OFAC sanctions for TRX addresses?+
Yes. Every TRXRiskScan query automatically cross-references the TRX address against the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list, EU financial sanctions lists, UN Security Council sanctions lists, UK HM Treasury list, and 40+ additional national and international watchlists. Results are updated continuously every 15 minutes as new sanctions designations are issued, ensuring you always act on current OFAC compliance data for TRON.
Can I check a TRON transaction hash for AML risk?+
Yes. Enter any TRON transaction hash (TXID) into the scanner to analyze both the sending and receiving TRX addresses of the transfer. TRXRiskScan identifies whether either party has prior exposure to illicit activity, is on a sanctions list, or has interacted with known high-risk entities on TRON. This is especially useful for exchanges and OTC desks screening incoming TRX deposits before crediting user accounts.
What is TRX fund source tracing and how far back does it go?+
TRX fund source tracing follows the origin of TRX and TRC20 tokens backward through the TRON blockchain transaction graph. TRXRiskScan traces up to 10 hops of transaction history by default, covering virtually all economically meaningful TRX fund flows on TRON. For each hop, it categorizes the source as a known exchange, OTC desk, darknet market, TRON mixer, P2P platform, or unknown wallet — revealing what percentage of TRX in a wallet is clean versus potentially tainted.
What is TRX mixer exposure and why does it matter for AML?+
Cryptocurrency mixers deliberately obfuscate the origin of TRX funds by pooling and redistributing tokens to break the on-chain transaction trail. OFAC and FinCEN treat mixer interaction as a significant AML red flag for potential money laundering. TRXRiskScan detects direct and indirect interactions with known TRON-based mixers and reports the exact percentage of TRX wallet funds exposed to mixing activity, enabling exchanges to apply risk-based controls accordingly.

TRXRiskScan's risk classification framework, data sources, and reporting formats are designed to satisfy FATF Recommendations 15 and 16, as well as jurisdiction-specific AML laws derived from FATF guidance for TRON network participants including exchanges and OTC desks handling TRX.

What is a TRX peeling chain and can TRXRiskScan detect it?+
A peeling chain is a TRON money laundering technique where TRX is moved through a long series of wallet addresses in rapid succession — each time sending a small amount to a peel address and the remainder forward — making funds difficult to trace. TRXRiskScan's graph analysis engine detects peeling chain patterns on TRON by identifying linear transaction sequences with characteristic output splitting ratios, flagging them as structural AML money laundering indicators in the TRX risk report.

Why is TRX a high-priority asset for AML compliance?+
TRX is the native currency of the TRON network and underpins billions of dollars in daily USDT TRC20 volume. Its extremely low transaction fees (often below $0.01) and fast settlement times make it attractive not only for legitimate payments but also for illicit actors seeking to move funds inexpensively at scale. Regulators globally have identified TRON-based assets including TRX as a high-risk vector for money laundering, sanctions evasion, and darknet market activity, making TRX risk scanning a critical compliance requirement for any VASP or exchange operating on TRON.
What is a TRON wallet AML check and who needs it?+
A TRON wallet AML check evaluates the risk profile of a specific TRX wallet address by analyzing its transaction history, fund sources, and counterparty exposures against known AML databases and sanctions lists. Crypto exchanges, OTC brokers, payment processors, DeFi platforms, and compliance officers all need TRON wallet AML checks to comply with FATF guidelines, avoid sanctions violations, prevent exposure to illicit TRX funds, and maintain regulatory licenses in their operating jurisdictions.
What is KYT and how does it relate to TRX risk scanning?+
KYT (Know Your Transaction) is the blockchain AML practice of monitoring and analyzing individual cryptocurrency transactions rather than just end-user identities. For TRX and TRON, KYT involves real-time screening of every deposit or withdrawal transaction to identify risk flags, sanctions exposure, and unusual fund flow patterns. TRXRiskScan provides KYT functionality for TRON by scoring both wallet addresses and transaction hashes, enabling exchanges to apply KYT across their entire TRX transaction flow in real time without additional compliance infrastructure.

What is a VASP and what TRX compliance obligations does it have?+
A Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) is any business that exchanges, transfers, or custodies cryptocurrencies including TRX or USDT TRC20. Under FATF Recommendation 15, VASPs must implement robust AML programs including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and sanctions screening. For TRON-based operations, this means running risk checks on every TRX deposit address, applying the Travel Rule to TRX transfers above threshold amounts, and maintaining audit-ready AML records — all of which TRXRiskScan facilitates.
What is the TRON Travel Rule and how does TRXRiskScan support it?+
The FATF Travel Rule requires VASPs to collect and transmit originator and beneficiary information for cryptocurrency transfers above $1,000 including TRX transactions on TRON. TRXRiskScan supports Travel Rule compliance by enabling pre-transaction risk screening of TRON counterparty wallets, identifying whether a receiving TRX address belongs to a regulated VASP or an unhosted wallet, and generating documentation needed for Travel Rule message matching systems used by TRON ecosystem participants and compliant exchanges.
Can TRXRiskScan detect structuring (smurfing) in TRX transactions?+
Yes. Structuring involves breaking large TRX transactions into many smaller amounts to avoid detection thresholds — a classic AML red flag known as smurfing. TRXRiskScan's behavioral analysis engine detects structuring patterns on TRON by identifying clusters of same-day, similarly-sized transactions from a network of coordinated TRX wallets. These patterns are flagged as high-risk AML indicators and contribute to the overall TRX risk score for the analyzed address.

TRXRiskScan maintains a curated database of cryptocurrency exchanges classified by their KYC and AML standards. Exchanges operating without identity verification are tagged as high-risk counterparties for TRX AML purposes. Any TRON wallet with significant TRX flows to or from these no-KYC platforms receives an elevated risk score. The Exchange KYC indicator in TRXRiskScan's risk panel shows whether a TRX address is primarily transacting through compliant, partial-KYC, or no-KYC venues on and off the TRON network.

What is round-tripping in TRON AML and how is it detected?+
Round-tripping is a TRON money laundering technique where TRX funds are sent through a chain of wallet addresses and eventually return to a wallet controlled by the same actor, creating the appearance of legitimate trading activity. TRXRiskScan's graph analysis engine identifies round-trip patterns by mapping closed-loop TRX fund flows in the TRON transaction graph where output paths converge back to known input clusters — a strong structural AML red flag on the TRON blockchain flagged in every risk report.
